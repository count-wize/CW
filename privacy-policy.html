Legal Basis for Processing

Under UK GDPR, we rely on the following lawful bases to process your personal data:

  • Contract performance -- Article 6(1)(b): processing your enquiry and case-intake data is necessary to take steps at your request before entering into a contract, or to perform a contract for on-chain tracing services.
  • Legitimate interests -- Article 6(1)(f): we process technical and analytics data (via Google Analytics 4) to understand how visitors use the Website and to improve our content and services. We have assessed that this interest does not override your rights or freedoms, given that analytics data is aggregated and pseudonymised.
  • Consent -- Article 6(1)(a): we process marketing and advertising cookies (Google Ads conversion tracking, Meta Pixel) only after you have given explicit consent via our cookie banner. You may withdraw consent at any time by adjusting your cookie preferences or contacting us.
  • Legal obligation -- Article 6(1)(c): where processing is necessary to comply with a legal obligation to which we are subject, such as tax or regulatory record-keeping.

Where we rely on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

Google Consent Mode v2

This Website uses Google Consent Mode v2 to manage how Google tags behave based on your cookie preferences. By default, analytics storage (analytics_storage) and advertising storage (ad_storage) are set to "denied". Google Analytics 4 and Google Ads conversion tags will fire in full only after you grant consent via the cookie banner. If you decline cookies, Google tags may still send cookieless pings (with no personal identifiers) to support aggregated, privacy-preserving measurement -- this is standard Consent Mode v2 behaviour as documented by Google.

Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law. Our retention periods are:

  • Enquiry and contact form data: 24 months from your last contact with us, after which it is securely deleted.
  • Client case data: 7 years from case closure, in line with statutory retention requirements (including tax, anti-money laundering, and professional-indemnity obligations).
  • Analytics raw data (Google Analytics 4): 14 months (the GA4 default retention setting), after which data is automatically aggregated and individual-level records are deleted by Google.
  • Cookie consent records: 12 months, after which the cookie banner will prompt you again for your preferences.

When personal data is no longer required, we delete or anonymise it securely.

International Data Transfers

Some of our processors are based outside the United Kingdom. In particular, Google LLC and Netlify Inc. are based in the United States. Where personal data is transferred to a country that is not covered by a UK adequacy decision, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with UK GDPR Chapter V.

For US-based processors, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) supplemented by the UK International Data Transfer Addendum (UK Addendum), as approved by the Information Commissioner's Office. These clauses impose contractual obligations on the recipient to protect your data to a standard equivalent to UK data-protection law.

For processors based in the European Economic Area (such as LiveChat Software S.A. in Poland), transfers are covered by the UK's adequacy regulations for EEA countries, meaning no additional transfer mechanism is currently required.

You may request a copy of the transfer safeguards we have in place by contacting us at privacy@countwize.co.

Your Data Protection Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access (Article 15): to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification (Article 16): to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure (Article 17): to request deletion of your data where there is no compelling reason for its continued processing.
  • Right to restriction (Article 18): to request that we restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability (Article 20): to receive your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format and to transmit it to another controller.
  • Right to object (Article 21): to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.
  • Right to withdraw consent: where processing is based on consent (Article 6(1)(a)), to withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at privacy@countwize.co. We will acknowledge your request within 5 business days and respond substantively within one calendar month, in accordance with UK GDPR Article 12.

There is no fee for exercising your rights in most circumstances. If your request is clearly unfounded or excessive, we may charge a reasonable fee or refuse the request, as permitted by law.
