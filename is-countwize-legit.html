What CountWize actually does — and does not do

Transparency about our scope is central to how we operate. Many people searching for help with crypto fraud encounter firms that make broad or vague claims. We prefer to be specific about both what our service includes and where it ends.

What we do

CountWize provides on-chain tracing: a forensic analysis of public blockchain data to map the movement of cryptocurrency from the victim's wallet or transaction to destination wallets and, where possible, to the regulated exchange where those funds were deposited. The output of our work is a written intelligence report that documents:

  • The transaction IDs (TXIDs) and wallet addresses involved in the loss event
  • The movement of funds across the blockchain, including any intermediate hops
  • Attribution of destination wallets to known exchanges or services, where that attribution is possible with reasonable confidence
  • A plain-language summary formatted for submission alongside an Action Fraud crime reference number

This report can also be used by a solicitor as the basis for a Norwich Pharmacal Order application — a UK court order compelling a regulated exchange to disclose the account-holder identity of the person who received the funds. For more detail on how tracing works, see our guide: How Crypto Tracing Works.

What we do not do

  • We do not guarantee asset recovery. Tracing identifies where funds went; it does not automatically result in the return of those funds. Whether tracing leads to any outcome depends on factors outside our control, including exchange cooperation, the jurisdiction of the fraudster, and the availability of legal mechanisms.
  • We do not provide financial advice or legal advice. Our reports are investigative documents. We are not solicitors, barristers, or financial advisers. For legal action based on our reports, you will need to instruct a qualified solicitor.
  • We do not hold, transfer, or manage cryptocurrency. We never ask clients to send cryptocurrency to us as part of our service. Any request to do so is a fraud red flag.
  • We are not an FCA-authorised firm. We do not offer financial products, take custody of assets, or provide regulated payment services. FCA authorisation is not required for the documentary tracing service we provide, and we do not claim otherwise.

Why we are not on the FCA register

Some people searching "is CountWize legit" check the FCA Financial Services Register and do not find us there. This is expected. The FCA register lists firms authorised to provide regulated financial services — things like investment advice, arranging deals in investments, or providing payment services. CountWize provides documentary on-chain tracing: we produce written forensic analysis reports. This is an investigative service, not a regulated financial service, and it does not require FCA authorisation. We disclose this prominently on every page of this site.

What you should check on the FCA register is whether a firm has been flagged. You can search by firm name at fca.org.uk/scamsmart. CountWize Ltd has not been flagged by the FCA. If you ever see us on the FCA Warning List, please contact us immediately through the details on this page — it would indicate an impersonator has been reported to the FCA using our name.

Independent review platforms

Who we cannot help

  • Your loss involved cash, bank transfers, or payment cards with no blockchain component — on-chain tracing requires on-chain transaction data to work from.
  • You have already paid a third-party firm claiming to offer recovery services and wish to recover those fees from us — this is a separate matter entirely outside our scope.
  • You are seeking a guarantee that our tracing will result in the return of your funds — we cannot make that promise and we will not make it.
  • The transaction occurred more than 5 years ago with no identifiable on-chain activity since — older transactions with no recent movement are significantly harder to pursue.
  • Your funds moved exclusively through privacy coins (such as Monero) with no exchange conversion points — privacy coin transactions are not fully traceable using current on-chain methods.
