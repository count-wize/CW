What happened to FTX and FTX UK in November 2022

On 11 November 2022, FTX Trading Ltd and its affiliated entities filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection in the United States District Court for the District of Delaware. The collapse followed a period of intense market scrutiny triggered by a public dispute between FTX founder Sam Bankman-Fried and the CEO of Binance, which led to a bank-run on FTX's exchange and the rapid collapse of the FTX.com trading platform.

At the time of collapse, FTX.com was one of the largest cryptocurrency exchanges in the world by trading volume. Millions of customers across more than 100 countries had assets on the platform. Post-collapse investigations by the US Department of Justice revealed that FTX had misappropriated customer funds, using deposits to fund investments by its affiliated trading arm Alameda Research. Sam Bankman-Fried was subsequently convicted of wire fraud and other charges in the United States in November 2023 and sentenced to 25 years in federal prison.

For UK users specifically, the picture was complicated by the existence of a separate UK-registered entity: FTX UK Ltd. This entity held a cryptoasset business registration with the Financial Conduct Authority under the Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017. When FTX collapsed, FTX UK Ltd was placed into special administration under a separate legal regime, creating two distinct sets of proceedings that UK customers may be affected by depending on which entity they had an account with.

The scale of the collapse

Independent investigations identified a shortfall of several billion dollars in customer assets across the FTX group. The FTX US estate eventually assembled a substantial pool of assets for distribution, including through the recovery of assets previously transferred by Bankman-Fried and other FTX insiders. As of 2025, the FTX estate had identified sufficient assets to propose a reorganisation plan that would pay all creditors 100% of their allowed claims as of the bankruptcy filing date, plus interest — a materially better outcome than initially anticipated in the immediate aftermath of the collapse.

However, it is important to understand that receiving the full allowed claim value does not mean creditors receive the current market value of their original cryptocurrency holdings. Claims are valued in US dollars at the time of the bankruptcy filing, not at any subsequent date. Given the significant increase in cryptocurrency prices since November 2022, many creditors will receive fewer dollars than the current market value of equivalent cryptocurrency holdings.

FTX UK Ltd versus FTX.com: why the distinction matters

The distinction between FTX UK Ltd and the broader FTX.com platform is critical to understanding which proceedings apply to your claim and where you need to file.

Most UK users who signed up and traded on FTX.com are creditors in the US Chapter 11 proceedings of FTX Trading Ltd, not creditors of FTX UK Ltd. The two entities maintained separate account databases, separate client funds (to the extent those funds were properly segregated — which they largely were not), and separate legal proceedings. If you are uncertain which entity held your account, you should check the terms of service you agreed to when opening your account and review any account statements or correspondence from FTX prior to the collapse.

FTX UK Ltd special administration

FTX UK Ltd was placed into special administration on 11 November 2022. The special administration regime under the Investment Bank Special Administration Regulations 2011 (IBSAR) is specifically designed for failed investment firms and has a primary objective of returning client assets to clients as quickly as possible. The FRP Advisory special administrators have been working through the FTX UK Ltd estate, which was significantly smaller than the broader FTX group.

The FCA has published guidance on the FTX UK Ltd special administration on its website. Creditors of FTX UK Ltd should monitor announcements from FRP Advisory directly and should have registered their claims through the FRP Advisory process. Updates are available at fca.org.uk and through the administrators' portal.

How the administration process works for UK creditors

UK insolvency law distinguishes between client assets (assets held on trust for clients, which do not form part of the estate available to general creditors) and unsecured creditor claims (monetary claims against the insolvent estate). The legal treatment of your FTX holdings depends significantly on how FTX held those assets and whether the holding arrangement constitutes a trust relationship under English law.

Client assets versus unsecured claims

For FTX UK Ltd, the IBSAR 2011 requires administrators to prioritise returning client assets — that is, assets held on trust for clients rather than as the firm's own assets. Whether cryptocurrency deposits at FTX UK were legally held on trust for clients is a matter that the administrators and potentially the courts will need to determine. If assets are found to have been held on trust, clients may be entitled to return of those specific assets (or equivalent value) ahead of general unsecured creditors. If assets are found to have been commingled with the firm's own assets, clients will be unsecured creditors.

This question of trust or no trust is not straightforward and will turn on detailed analysis of the contractual terms, actual asset-handling practices, and FCA client-asset rules. The outcome is not yet finally determined and may vary between FTX UK Ltd and the broader FTX group entities.

The creditor claim process under IBSAR

In special administration proceedings, creditors are required to submit a proof of debt — a formal document setting out the basis and quantum of their claim — to the administrators. For FTX UK Ltd, this process has been managed through the FRP Advisory portal. Creditors should:

  • Register their claim with the administrators as soon as possible if they have not already done so.
  • Provide documentary evidence of their account balance at the date of administration, including transaction history and account statements.
  • Monitor FRP Advisory's announcements for updates on the progress of the administration and any distribution proposals.
  • Take legal advice if their claim is objected to or if they disagree with the value assigned to their claim.

If you are a creditor of the broader FTX.com platform (FTX Trading Ltd), you are subject to the US proceedings and the Kroll claims portal, not the FRP Advisory process. The two processes are entirely separate.

The US Chapter 11 proceedings and what they mean for UK users

Chapter 11 bankruptcy in the United States is a reorganisation process, not simply a liquidation. Under Chapter 11, a debtor proposes a plan of reorganisation that must be approved by creditors and confirmed by the bankruptcy court. For FTX Trading Ltd, the reorganisation plan was filed by the estate in 2024 and subsequently confirmed by the court.

The FTX reorganisation plan

The key features of the confirmed FTX reorganisation plan relevant to UK creditors include:

  • Full repayment of allowed claims: The plan provides for repayment of 100% of allowed claim value, denominated in US dollars as of the bankruptcy filing date (November 2022).
  • Post-petition interest: The plan also provides for post-petition interest on allowed claims, recognising that creditors were denied access to their funds for an extended period.
  • Distribution classes: Claims are organised into classes. Customers with smaller claims (the convenience class, generally claims under $50,000) were prioritised for earlier distribution. Larger claims are processed through the main customer class.
  • Dollar-denominated distribution: Distributions are made in dollars (via wire transfer to a bank account), not in cryptocurrency. This is significant given the substantial increase in cryptocurrency prices since the filing date.

Dollar valuation and the opportunity cost

One of the most significant practical issues for FTX creditors is that claims are valued at November 2022 prices. Bitcoin, for example, was trading at approximately $16,000 to $17,000 at the time of the FTX collapse and has traded significantly higher in subsequent years. A creditor who held Bitcoin on FTX will receive the dollar value of their Bitcoin as of November 2022, not the current market value. This is a standard feature of US bankruptcy law and is not unique to FTX. It represents a real and material economic loss for many creditors beyond the recovery they will receive through the bankruptcy process.

This issue has generated significant discussion in the FTX creditor community and in legal scholarship. Some creditors have argued that the dollar-valuation approach fails to account for the unique nature of cryptocurrency as property. However, the bankruptcy court has confirmed the dollar-denominated approach, and it is the framework within which distributions will be made.

Claim deadline and KYC requirements

The deadline for filing claims in the FTX US bankruptcy has passed. Creditors who had not filed claims by the bar date may have difficulty participating in distributions. However, the claims agent (Kroll) continues to operate the portal for ongoing claims administration, dispute resolution, and distribution processing. Creditors must also complete KYC (know your customer) verification through the Kroll portal before receiving any distribution. Failure to complete KYC will delay or prevent distribution.

UK creditors should ensure that their KYC submission to Kroll is complete and that their bank account details for distribution are accurate and current. Distribution instructions can be updated through the Kroll portal at restructuring.ra.kroll.com/FTX.

Filing a creditor claim: what the process involves

If you had assets on FTX.com and have not yet filed a claim, or if you have filed a claim and are managing the ongoing process, the following outlines the key steps and considerations.

Identifying which entity you are a creditor of

Before anything else, confirm whether you are a creditor of FTX UK Ltd (UK special administration) or FTX Trading Ltd and affiliated entities (US Chapter 11). The distinction is based on which entity you held your account with. Most UK users who accessed FTX via the main FTX.com website are creditors in the US proceedings. Users who specifically used the FTX UK regulated service (which offered a more limited product set than FTX.com) may be creditors of FTX UK Ltd in the English proceedings.

Gathering your evidence

Whether you are in the UK or US proceedings, supporting documentation strengthens your claim. The documentation you should gather includes:

  • Account statements or screenshots showing your balance as of the bankruptcy filing date (11 November 2022 for FTX Trading Ltd; the same date for FTX UK Ltd).
  • Transaction history showing deposits, withdrawals, and trading activity.
  • Any correspondence with FTX customer support.
  • Proof of identity as used when opening the account (important for KYC verification).
  • Any emails or notifications from FTX or the administrators regarding your account.

Where transaction records are incomplete — because FTX's own systems did not fully record history, or because your account was affected by the collapse before statements could be downloaded — on-chain transaction records from the blockchain itself may be relevant. For deposits to FTX from external wallets, the blockchain provides an independent record of the transfer that does not depend on FTX's internal records.

The claims portal process

For US proceedings, claims were filed through the Kroll portal. The portal required: account email address, claim amount, supporting documentation, and KYC verification. Ongoing management of the claim — including receiving distribution notifications and updating banking details — is also handled through the same portal.

For UK proceedings (FTX UK Ltd), claims were submitted to FRP Advisory. Creditors should have received communications from the special administrators and should contact FRP Advisory directly if they have not been contacted and believe they are a creditor of the UK entity.

Disputed claims

The FTX estate and its advisers have reviewed filed claims and in some cases issued objections. Common grounds for objection include: duplicate claims, claims without adequate supporting documentation, and claims that the estate believes are inaccurate in value. If your claim has been objected to, you will need to respond through the formal objection process, providing additional documentation or legal argument to support your claim. This is a situation in which taking specialist legal advice — from a solicitor with experience in US bankruptcy proceedings or from a specialist creditor rights firm — is strongly advisable.

What UK creditors can realistically expect in 2026

As of April 2026, the FTX proceedings have reached a point where distributions are occurring, though the timeline and completeness of distributions vary by creditor class and claim status.

Current distribution status

The confirmed reorganisation plan has enabled the FTX estate to begin distributing funds to creditors. Convenience class creditors (claims under $50,000) were the first to receive distributions, with the plan providing for prompt payment of these smaller claims. Customers in the main creditor class with larger claims are at various stages of processing, depending on whether their claims have been allowed, whether disputes have been resolved, and whether KYC is complete.

Creditors should not expect a single large payment event. The distribution process is phased and will continue over an extended period. Some creditors will have already received their distribution; others are still in claim processing or dispute resolution stages.

The dollar-versus-crypto issue in practice

Given that cryptocurrency markets have substantially recovered and in many cases exceeded November 2022 price levels, the dollar-denominated nature of distributions is a material consideration for UK creditors in 2026. A creditor who held a diversified portfolio on FTX will receive the November 2022 dollar value of that portfolio — which for many assets represents a fraction of what that portfolio would be worth at current prices if the assets had been held elsewhere.

This outcome is legally correct under US bankruptcy law and is not a basis for any further claim against the FTX estate. Creditors who wish to consider whether any legal avenue exists to challenge the valuation methodology should take specialist legal advice. However, it should be noted that the bankruptcy court confirmed the plan including this valuation methodology, and any challenge at this stage would face significant procedural obstacles.

FTX UK Ltd administration progress

For creditors of FTX UK Ltd specifically, the special administration continues under FRP Advisory's oversight. Progress updates are published by FRP Advisory and by the FCA. UK creditors who are in the FTX UK Ltd proceedings should monitor FRP Advisory's announcements and ensure their contact details and claims are up to date. The FCA has maintained a dedicated webpage for FTX UK Ltd updates at fca.org.uk.

Realistic timelines

Large-scale insolvency proceedings of this complexity routinely extend over multiple years. While the FTX reorganisation plan has been confirmed and distributions are occurring, the full administration is unlikely to be concluded quickly. Some creditors — particularly those with disputed claims, complex claim histories, or incomplete KYC — may wait significantly longer than those with straightforward, undisputed claims who have completed all procedural requirements.

If you have not received any communication from the FTX claims process and believe you should be a creditor, contact the Kroll claims agent or FRP Advisory directly, depending on which entity you had an account with.

How on-chain tracing can support a creditor claim

For most FTX creditors, the primary path to recovering the value of their lost holdings is through the official claims process described above. On-chain tracing does not create an alternative route to recovering funds outside that process. However, there are specific circumstances in which on-chain tracing can support or strengthen a creditor's position.

Reconstructing transaction history

If your FTX account records are incomplete or disputed, on-chain tracing can provide an independent record of transactions that occurred on the blockchain. When you deposited cryptocurrency to FTX from an external wallet, that deposit transaction is permanently recorded on the relevant blockchain. A tracing exercise can identify the transaction IDs, amounts, timestamps, and destination addresses for those deposits, providing objective documentary evidence that does not depend on FTX's internal systems.

This can be valuable in situations where: the FTX claims portal shows a different balance from what you believe you held; you need to demonstrate a specific deposit was received by FTX; or you are supporting a dispute with the estate over the value of your claim.

Investigating transactions before the collapse

For creditors who are concerned that FTX moved their specific assets in the period leading up to the collapse — for example, by transferring assets to Alameda Research or to external wallets — on-chain tracing can document what happened to assets after they arrived at FTX's known wallet addresses. This is unlikely to change the outcome of the insolvency proceedings for individual creditors (the proceedings deal with the overall shortfall, not individual asset movements), but it may inform decisions about whether to support or oppose specific aspects of the plan, or whether to participate in any proceedings related to recovery of fraudulently transferred assets.

It is important to be realistic: on-chain tracing by an individual creditor is not a substitute for the large-scale forensic work being conducted by the FTX estate, its advisers, and US law enforcement. The Department of Justice and the FTX estate have engaged specialist blockchain analytics firms with significant resources and data access that go beyond what is available in individual tracing work.

Warning: FTX-related scams targeting creditors

Fraud warning: FTX creditors are a known target for scammers. Any unsolicited contact offering to manage your FTX claim, recover your FTX funds, or accelerate your distribution in exchange for a fee should be treated as a potential scam. These contacts may claim to be from law firms, recovery specialists, or even impersonate official administrators or regulators. Report any such contact to Action Fraud and to the FCA ScamSmart register.

The collapse of FTX created an unusually large pool of distressed creditors, many of whom had lost significant sums and were urgently seeking any path to recovery. Fraudsters have exploited this by running targeted scams that specifically target FTX creditors. The common patterns include:

Fake recovery firms

Fraudsters posing as specialist "crypto recovery" firms contact FTX creditors — often via social media, email, or Telegram — claiming to have connections to the FTX estate or to FTX insiders that will allow them to recover funds more quickly or more completely than the official process. They charge upfront fees (described as "processing fees", "legal fees", or "compliance charges") and then disappear or invent further charges.

There is no legitimate shortcut to the FTX claims process. No private firm can accelerate your distribution through the official proceedings. Any firm that claims otherwise and asks for money is a scam. See our full guide on how to spot fake recovery firms for detailed warning signs.

Impersonation of official administrators

Some fraudsters impersonate FRP Advisory (the FTX UK Ltd administrators) or Kroll (the FTX US claims agent) by creating fake websites, sending emails from spoofed domains, or setting up fake social media profiles. They may direct creditors to fake portals that collect personal information or banking details.

Always verify the identity of anyone who contacts you about your FTX claim by checking official contact details directly from the real websites: frpadvisory.com (FTX UK Ltd administrators) and restructuring.ra.kroll.com/FTX (FTX US claims agent). Never click links in unsolicited emails — navigate directly to these sites.

Fake FTX token claims

Various fraudulent schemes have emerged offering FTX creditors the ability to sell their claims at a discount, receive "FTX tokens" as compensation, or participate in fake claim-settlement pools. None of these are legitimate components of the official proceedings. The FTX estate distributes only in US dollars through the official Kroll portal; it does not issue tokens or operate claim-trading schemes.

Secondary market claim trading — legitimate versus fake

There is a legitimate secondary market in which creditors can sell their FTX claims to third-party investors at a discount to face value. This allows creditors who need immediate liquidity to receive some value now rather than waiting for the full distribution timeline. Legitimate claim-trading platforms include established distressed-debt investors and some specialist firms. However, this market also attracts fraudulent operators. If you are considering selling your claim, verify any buyer's identity, use a written agreement reviewed by a solicitor, and never pay an upfront fee to a claim buyer — legitimate buyers pay you, not the other way around.

Frequently asked questions

Am I an FTX creditor if I had funds on FTX.com but I am in the UK?

Most UK users who accessed FTX via FTX.com are creditors in the US Chapter 11 bankruptcy proceedings of FTX Trading Ltd, not creditors of FTX UK Ltd (the FCA-registered UK entity). The distinction depends on which entity your account was contractually with. UK users of the UK-specific regulated service are creditors of FTX UK Ltd; UK users who signed up directly on FTX.com are generally creditors in the US proceedings. If you are uncertain, review your account terms of service or contact the relevant administrator.

Has the FTX estate paid out anything to creditors yet?

Yes. The FTX reorganisation plan was confirmed in October 2024, and distributions have begun for customers in the convenience class (claims under $50,000), with the plan providing 100% of allowed claim value plus post-petition interest. Larger claims are being processed. Timelines depend on the creditor's claim class, whether the claim has been objected to, and KYC compliance. Monitor the Kroll portal for updates on your specific claim status.

What happened to FTX UK Ltd specifically?

FTX UK Ltd was the FCA-registered entity that held a cryptoasset business registration. It was placed into special administration under the Investment Bank Special Administration Regulations 2011. The special administrators from FRP Advisory have been managing the estate and creditor process. FTX UK Ltd is a separate legal entity from FTX.com, and its administration is a distinct process. UK creditors of this entity should monitor FRP Advisory announcements directly.

Why am I receiving the dollar value of my holdings rather than my actual crypto back?

In insolvency proceedings, creditors generally receive monetary claims rather than specific assets, unless client assets were legally held on trust and can be identified and returned. For FTX, the reorganisation plan distributes dollar-denominated values as of the bankruptcy filing date in November 2022. This is standard US bankruptcy treatment. Since cryptocurrency prices have generally increased significantly since November 2022, this means many creditors receive fewer dollars than the current market equivalent of their original holdings.

Can on-chain tracing help FTX creditors?

On-chain tracing can document the blockchain record of your deposits to FTX, which may be useful if your claim value is disputed or incomplete. It cannot change the outcome of the insolvency proceedings or provide a route to recover funds outside the official process. CountWize can produce a written intelligence report documenting your transaction history for use in supporting your creditor claim or in discussion with a solicitor. We do not guarantee any outcome.

Are there legitimate services that help FTX creditors with their claims?

The official claims agent for the US proceedings is Kroll (restructuring.ra.kroll.com). Specialist insolvency solicitors and restructuring advisers can advise on claims strategy and dispute resolution. Be very cautious of unsolicited contacts offering to manage your FTX claim for a fee — this is a known fraud pattern. Any firm asking for an upfront payment to help with your FTX claim outside the official process should be treated as a potential scam and reported to Action Fraud.

We will never ask for: your seed phrase, private key, exchange password, two-factor codes, remote access to your computer, or cryptocurrency payment to begin work. Any message claiming to be from CountWize and asking for these is a scam. Report it to us on the number above and to actionfraud.police.uk.
