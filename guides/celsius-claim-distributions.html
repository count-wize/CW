What happened to Celsius Network and why it collapsed

Celsius Network LLC filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection in the United States Bankruptcy Court for the Southern District of New York on 13 July 2022. At the time of its collapse, Celsius was one of the largest centralised cryptocurrency lending platforms in the world, with approximately $12 billion in assets under management and more than a million active accounts. The platform had attracted retail depositors with offers of unusually high interest rates — sometimes exceeding 17% annually — on cryptocurrency deposits.

The collapse was triggered by a combination of factors: a broader cryptocurrency market downturn in mid-2022, a liquidity crisis driven by significant exposure to illiquid assets and over-leveraged lending positions, and losses linked to the collapse of the TerraLUNA ecosystem in May 2022. On 12 June 2022 — four weeks before the bankruptcy filing — Celsius froze all withdrawals, swaps, and transfers on its platform, leaving over a million customers unable to access their funds. The company cited "extreme market conditions" as the reason for the freeze.

Subsequent investigations by the bankruptcy estate and by US regulators revealed that Celsius had operated its business in ways that differed materially from how it was presented to customers. Customer deposits in the Earn programme were not held separately from Celsius's own assets and were actively deployed in speculative activities by Celsius's trading teams, including lending to institutional counterparties, deploying in decentralised finance (DeFi) protocols, and investing in illiquid positions. When the market turned and counterparties began recalling loans, Celsius did not have sufficient liquid assets to meet customer withdrawal demands.

The scale of the shortfall

The bankruptcy filing revealed a shortfall of approximately $1.2 billion between Celsius's assets and its liabilities to customers. This shortfall — the gap between what Celsius owed to depositors and what it could realise from its asset portfolio — determined the ultimate recovery rate for unsecured creditors. The final distribution outcomes depended on the estate's ability to realise value from illiquid assets, including Bitcoin mining operations, cryptocurrency portfolios, and litigation claims against third parties.

Criminal investigations followed. Former Celsius CEO Alex Mashinsky was arrested in July 2023 and charged with securities fraud, commodities fraud, wire fraud, and market manipulation. His trial and conviction in December 2024 — he pleaded guilty to charges of securities fraud and price manipulation — represented a significant outcome for regulators and creditors seeking accountability for the platform's conduct. His sentencing occurred in May 2025.

The regulatory picture for UK customers

Celsius was not FCA-authorised and did not hold any UK regulatory permissions. It operated in the UK as an unregulated entity offering cryptocurrency interest products to UK retail customers. This means UK customers of Celsius had no access to the Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS) protections that apply to deposits with FCA-authorised firms, and no recourse through the Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS). UK customers are unsecured creditors in the US bankruptcy proceedings — there is no parallel UK insolvency process for Celsius, unlike the FTX situation which had a separate UK-registered entity.

The FCA had repeatedly warned about the risks of unregulated cryptocurrency interest platforms before and after the Celsius collapse. The FCA's ScamSmart resource and its register of unauthorised firms remain useful tools for UK consumers verifying the regulatory status of any platform. See fca.org.uk/scamsmart.

Celsius account types and how they affected creditor treatment

One of the most significant and contentious aspects of the Celsius bankruptcy was the different legal treatment applied to different account types. Celsius operated several account products, and the bankruptcy court's rulings on the nature of each account type had a material effect on what creditors received and when.

The Earn account ruling and its consequences

The most consequential ruling in the Celsius bankruptcy was the court's determination that Celsius Earn account holders had transferred title to their cryptocurrency to Celsius under the terms of service they had agreed to. This meant Earn account holders were unsecured creditors of the Celsius estate — they were owed a monetary amount, not entitled to the return of specific assets held on trust for them.

This ruling, delivered by US Bankruptcy Judge Martin Glenn in January 2023, was deeply significant for the majority of Celsius creditors, who were Earn account holders. It meant that their distributions would be paid from the general estate — after secured creditors and administrative expenses — and that their claims were valued in dollars at the bankruptcy filing date, not at current market prices.

The Custody account distinction

By contrast, the court found that assets in Celsius Custody accounts were held on trust for customers, meaning those assets had not legally transferred to Celsius and were client property rather than estate property. Custody account holders were therefore entitled to the return of their specific assets (or equivalent cryptocurrency) ahead of the general creditor distribution process. This distinction produced a meaningfully different outcome for the two groups and generated significant debate about the adequacy of Celsius's disclosures to customers about the different account types.

Many customers had not clearly distinguished between Earn and Custody accounts and were unaware that transferring assets to an Earn account changed the legal ownership of those assets. This lack of clear disclosure was a significant regulatory and legal issue addressed in the proceedings and in subsequent enforcement actions.

The Celsius reorganisation plan: key terms explained

The Celsius Chapter 11 reorganisation plan was confirmed by the bankruptcy court on 9 November 2023. The plan set out the structure and terms under which the Celsius estate's remaining assets would be distributed to creditors. The confirmed plan was the result of an extended negotiation process involving the official committee of unsecured creditors, the bankruptcy estate, and various creditor groups.

Dollar valuation at the petition date

Like other major cryptocurrency exchange bankruptcy proceedings, the Celsius reorganisation plan values creditor claims in US dollars as of the bankruptcy petition date — 13 July 2022 — not at the date of distribution. This is a standard feature of US bankruptcy law and means that creditors receive the dollar equivalent of their cryptocurrency holdings at July 2022 prices, not current market prices.

The practical effect of this valuation methodology was significant. Many major cryptocurrencies were trading at substantially lower prices in July 2022 than in subsequent years, following the broader crypto market cycle. A creditor who held Bitcoin on Celsius at the filing date received a dollar claim based on Bitcoin's approximately $20,000 price in July 2022 — not its price at the time of distribution, which was considerably higher in 2024 and 2025. This represents a real economic loss beyond the insolvency shortfall itself.

The NewCo structure: Ionic Digital Inc.

A distinctive feature of the Celsius reorganisation was the creation of a new entity — referred to as NewCo during the proceedings and subsequently incorporated as Ionic Digital Inc. — to hold Celsius's Bitcoin mining operations. Rather than liquidating these assets immediately, the reorganisation plan transferred the mining assets to NewCo and distributed equity in NewCo to eligible Earn creditors as part of their distribution package.

This meant that Earn creditors received a mix of liquid assets (Bitcoin and Ether) and illiquid NewCo equity. The value of NewCo equity at the time of distribution was uncertain and depends on the operational performance of the mining business and any future liquidity events. Creditors who received NewCo equity are, in effect, equity holders in a new Bitcoin mining company — their ability to realise that value depends on the company's future prospects.

Recovery percentages

The overall recovery percentage for Earn creditors under the confirmed plan was approximately 67 cents to 72 cents on the dollar (on the petition-date dollar valuation), with the exact figure varying by creditor class, the timing of distributions, and the realised value of NewCo equity. This figure should be understood in the context that it represents a percentage of petition-date values, not current market values — for many creditors, the economic loss expressed at current cryptocurrency prices was considerably larger.

Custody account holders, as discussed above, received their assets in kind and were not subject to the general recovery percentage applicable to Earn creditors.

How the Celsius distribution process works

The Celsius distribution process is administered by Stretto, the court-appointed claims and noticing agent. All creditor interactions — claim filing, KYC verification, distribution instructions, and status tracking — are managed through the Stretto portal at cases.stretto.com/celsius.

KYC and distribution prerequisites

Before receiving any distribution, creditors must complete a multi-step process through the Stretto portal:

  • Identity verification (KYC): Creditors must provide government-issued identification and pass identity verification checks consistent with anti-money-laundering (AML) requirements. Failure to complete KYC prevents distribution.
  • Distribution instructions: Creditors must specify how they wish to receive their distribution — whether by wire transfer to a bank account (for cash distributions), by providing a cryptocurrency wallet address (for Bitcoin or Ether distributions), or through the relevant brokerage or platform for NewCo equity distributions.
  • Tax forms: US creditors must provide W-9 forms; non-US creditors (including UK creditors) must provide W-8BEN forms certifying their non-US tax status. Failure to provide tax documentation can delay distribution.

UK creditors submitting W-8BEN forms should take care to complete these correctly. Errors on W-8BEN forms can result in withholding tax being applied or distribution being delayed. If uncertain, seek guidance from a tax adviser familiar with US non-resident tax obligations.

Distribution tranches

The Celsius estate has distributed assets in multiple tranches since the plan became effective in early 2024. Not all creditors receive distributions at the same time; the process is phased. The first distributions prioritised Custody account holders. Subsequent tranches have addressed Earn account creditors in stages, with the composition of each tranche (the mix of cash, Bitcoin, Ether, and NewCo equity) determined by the estate's asset realisation progress at the time.

Creditors should not expect their entire allowed claim to be distributed in a single event. The process involves multiple payments over time, and the final tranche of distributions may not occur until all estate administration is concluded.

Disputed and unresolved claims

Some Celsius creditors' claims remain subject to dispute or objection by the estate. The estate has the right to object to claims that it believes are inaccurate, unsupported, or duplicative. Creditors who receive a claim objection notice have a limited time to respond. Responding to a claim objection typically requires providing supporting documentation — account statements, transaction records, and any other evidence establishing the basis and quantum of the claim.

If your claim remains disputed, you will not receive distributions on the disputed portion until the dispute is resolved, either by agreement with the estate or through a court ruling. Taking specialist legal advice from a solicitor with experience in US bankruptcy claims is advisable if you are facing a claim objection.

What creditors have received: cash, crypto, and NewCo equity

By 2025 and into 2026, the Celsius distribution process has progressed through multiple tranches. The specific assets distributed in each tranche have varied, reflecting the estate's asset realisation progress and the confirmed plan terms. This section summarises the general structure of what creditors have received; it is not legal or financial advice, and the exact entitlements of any individual creditor depend on their specific account type, claim class, and claim status.

Custody account holders

Custody account holders received priority treatment under the plan. The estate returned custody assets in cryptocurrency — that is, in the specific digital assets held in those accounts (or equivalent amounts of the same assets). This was materially more favourable than the treatment of Earn creditors and reflected the court's finding that custody assets were client property rather than estate property. Many custody account holders completed their distributions in 2024.

Earn account creditors

Earn account creditors received their distributions in a combination of forms determined by the plan's mechanics:

  • Bitcoin (BTC): A portion of each Earn creditor's allowed claim was distributed in Bitcoin, transferred to a wallet address provided by the creditor.
  • Ether (ETH): A portion was distributed in Ether, similarly transferred to a creditor-specified wallet.
  • NewCo equity (Ionic Digital Inc.): A portion was distributed as equity in the reorganised mining entity. This equity was initially distributed through a designated brokerage arrangement. Its value fluctuates and is not readily liquid in the same way as cryptocurrency.
  • Cash (USD): Some tranches included cash distributions, particularly for smaller claims or where specific assets had been realised by the estate.

The proportions of each asset type in the distribution depended on the creditor's specific claim class and the terms agreed in the confirmed plan. Creditors should refer to their plan confirmation documents and Stretto portal for the specific breakdown applicable to their claim.

Ongoing residual distributions

As of 2026, the Celsius estate continues to wind down and may make further residual distributions as additional assets are realised — including proceeds from litigation, asset sales, and resolution of outstanding claims. The estate has pursued litigation against former Celsius executives and third parties involved in the company's conduct prior to the bankruptcy, and any proceeds from such litigation may generate additional distributions. Creditors should monitor official communications from the estate and the Stretto portal for updates on further distributions.

Celsius and UK creditors: specific considerations

UK creditors of Celsius face a number of practical and legal considerations that are specific to their situation as non-US participants in a US bankruptcy process.

No UK insolvency parallel process

Unlike FTX, which had a UK-registered subsidiary (FTX UK Ltd) that was placed into a separate UK special administration process, Celsius had no UK-registered entity. All UK Celsius creditors are participants in the US Chapter 11 proceedings. There is no parallel UK insolvency process, no UK administrator to contact, and no UK-specific claims portal. All claim management, KYC, and distribution instructions must be handled through the Stretto portal for the US proceedings.

Tax treatment of distributions

The tax treatment of Celsius distributions for UK creditors is a complex area governed by UK tax law, specifically the rules on capital gains tax on cryptocurrency disposals and the treatment of capital losses arising from the insolvency. Creditors should take specialist advice from a UK tax adviser familiar with cryptocurrency taxation. HMRC has published general guidance on cryptoasset taxation, available at gov.uk, but this guidance does not specifically address the treatment of insolvency distributions, which involves additional complexity. The interaction of UK capital gains tax, the valuation of distributions in cryptocurrency and equity, and the treatment of capital losses all require specialist attention.

W-8BEN completion

UK creditors are required to complete and submit a W-8BEN form to certify their non-US status and claim applicable treaty benefits under the US-UK tax treaty. The W-8BEN form must be completed accurately; errors can result in the estate withholding US tax from distributions at the default withholding rate (currently 30%) rather than the reduced treaty rate. If you have not completed or updated your W-8BEN in the Stretto portal, this should be a priority action.

Currency conversion

Distributions made in US dollars will be received by UK creditors in USD and will need to be converted to GBP if required. Cash wire distributions require a bank account capable of receiving USD international wires. Cryptocurrency distributions — in Bitcoin or Ether — are received in the creditor's specified wallet and converted at the creditor's discretion. UK creditors should consider the exchange rate implications and any bank fees associated with receiving and converting US dollar distributions.

FSCS and FOS: no protection available

As noted above, Celsius was not FCA-authorised, and UK customers have no recourse to the Financial Services Compensation Scheme or the Financial Ombudsman Service in relation to their Celsius losses. This is one of the key risks associated with using unregulated cryptocurrency platforms. UK regulatory guidance on this is available from the FCA at fca.org.uk. For information on identifying regulated firms, see the FCA register.

How on-chain tracing can support a Celsius creditor claim

For most Celsius creditors, the primary path to recovering value from their holdings is through the official claims process described above. On-chain tracing does not create an alternative route to recovering funds outside the bankruptcy proceedings. However, there are specific circumstances in which on-chain tracing can support or strengthen a creditor's position within the official process.

Reconstructing deposit history

When you deposited cryptocurrency to Celsius from an external wallet — a hardware wallet, a software wallet, or another exchange — that transaction was permanently recorded on the relevant blockchain. On-chain tracing can identify and document those transactions: the transaction IDs, amounts, timestamps, originating wallet addresses, and Celsius deposit addresses. This provides an independent record that does not depend on Celsius's internal systems.

This type of tracing is relevant if: the balance shown on the Stretto claims portal differs from what you believe you deposited; you need to demonstrate a specific deposit occurred and the amount; you are supporting a dispute with the estate over the value of your claim; or your Celsius account records are incomplete because you lost access to the platform before downloading statements.

Documenting the on-chain trail after deposit

It is technically possible to trace, on a block-explorer level, where cryptocurrency went after it was received at Celsius's deposit addresses. This does not necessarily mean it is possible to follow individual funds through Celsius's internal commingling — because Celsius routinely moved funds between wallets and aggregated depositor funds — but it can document what happened to assets at Celsius's known wallet addresses in the period leading up to and immediately after the freeze. This type of analysis may be of interest to creditors who wish to understand the broader context of how their assets were handled, or to those supporting litigation efforts against former Celsius executives.

Warning: Celsius-related scams targeting creditors

Fraud warning: Celsius creditors are a known target for fraud. Any unsolicited contact offering to accelerate your Celsius distribution, manage your claim for a fee, or recover your Celsius funds outside the official process should be treated as a potential scam. Report any such contact to Action Fraud and to the FCA ScamSmart register.

The Celsius collapse created a large pool of distressed creditors, many of whom lost significant sums and were urgently seeking ways to recover their funds or expedite their distribution. This has made Celsius creditors an attractive target for fraud. The common patterns include:

Fake recovery firms

Fraudsters posing as specialist cryptocurrency tracing or recovery firms contact Celsius creditors — often via social media, Telegram, email, or WhatsApp — claiming to have connections to the Celsius estate, to former Celsius employees, or to blockchain forensics resources that can unlock your funds. They charge upfront fees described as "processing fees", "compliance deposits", or "legal retainers" and then either disappear or invent further charges.

There is no legitimate method for a private firm to recover your Celsius funds outside the official bankruptcy process. No private company can accelerate your Stretto distribution or extract assets from the Celsius estate. Any firm that claims otherwise and asks for money is operating a scam. See our guide on frequently asked questions about cryptocurrency tracing for detailed warning signs.

Impersonation of Stretto and official administrators

Fraudsters have created fake websites, email addresses, and social media profiles impersonating Stretto, the Celsius estate, or official administrators. They direct creditors to fake portals that collect personal data, KYC documents, and banking details for identity theft and fraud.

Always access the Celsius claims portal directly by typing cases.stretto.com/celsius into your browser — never via a link in an unsolicited email or message. Verify any communication claiming to be from the Celsius estate by checking it against official communications published on the Stretto portal.

Second-victim fraud

A particularly harmful pattern is "second-victim" or "recovery" fraud, in which a fraudster specifically targets people who have already lost money in a cryptocurrency insolvency by offering to help them get it back. If you have already paid a fraudulent firm claiming to assist with your Celsius claim, this does not exclude you from the legitimate claims process, but it does mean you have been the victim of a second fraud that should be independently reported to Action Fraud.

Fake Celsius token and relaunch schemes

Various schemes have circulated offering Celsius creditors the ability to receive "CEL token" compensation, participate in a Celsius relaunch, or access special funds outside the official proceedings. None of these are legitimate components of the confirmed reorganisation plan. The Celsius bankruptcy estate does not distribute CEL tokens and does not operate any "relaunch" scheme. Any offer along these lines is a fraud.

Frequently asked questions

Has Celsius started distributing funds to creditors?

Yes. The Celsius reorganisation plan was confirmed in November 2023, and distributions have been occurring in multiple tranches since early 2024. The process is managed through the Stretto claims portal at cases.stretto.com/celsius. Creditors must have completed KYC and provided distribution instructions to receive payments. The timing and form of individual distributions depends on account type, claim class, and whether all procedural requirements have been met.

How are Celsius creditor claims valued?

Celsius creditor claims are generally valued in US dollars as of the bankruptcy petition date (13 July 2022), not at current market prices. This is standard US bankruptcy treatment. Since many cryptocurrencies have traded significantly higher in 2024 and 2025 than their July 2022 values, creditors typically receive a dollar amount that is lower than the current market equivalent of their original holdings. The bankruptcy court confirmed this valuation methodology as part of the reorganisation plan.

What is the difference between an Earn account and a Custody account at Celsius?

The bankruptcy court found that Earn account holders had transferred title to their cryptocurrency to Celsius under the terms of service — making them unsecured creditors receiving monetary distributions. Custody account holders, by contrast, were found to hold assets on trust with Celsius, entitling them to in-kind return of their assets with priority over the general creditor pool. This distinction produced materially different distribution outcomes for the two groups.

What is NewCo (Ionic Digital) and what should I do with my equity?

Ionic Digital Inc. (NewCo) is a Bitcoin mining company created from Celsius's mining assets as part of the reorganisation plan. Eligible Earn creditors received a portion of their distribution as equity in Ionic Digital. The value of this equity is uncertain and not guaranteed. Creditors who received Ionic Digital equity should monitor the company's performance and any announcements about liquidity events or secondary market trading opportunities. This is not financial advice — decisions about holding or selling equity should be taken with independent professional advice.

Can on-chain tracing help me with my Celsius claim?

On-chain tracing can document the blockchain record of deposits you made to Celsius from external wallets, providing independent evidence of your transaction history. This may be useful if your claim balance is disputed or incomplete. CountWize can produce a written intelligence report documenting your deposit history for use in supporting your creditor claim or in discussion with a solicitor. We cannot change the legal outcome of the proceedings or provide access to Celsius's internal records. We do not guarantee any outcome.

I have not received my Celsius distribution — what should I do?

Log in to the Stretto portal (cases.stretto.com/celsius) and check your claim status. Common reasons for non-receipt include: KYC incomplete, banking or wallet details not submitted or incorrect, claim still under review, or claim in a later distribution tranche. Do not respond to unsolicited communications claiming to be about your distribution — navigate directly to the official Stretto portal. If you believe your claim is complete and you have received no updates, contact Stretto through the portal's official support channel.

Are there scams targeting Celsius creditors?

Yes. Celsius creditors are a specific fraud target. Common scams include fake recovery firms charging upfront fees, impersonation of Stretto and official administrators, and fake Celsius relaunch or token schemes. No private firm can legitimately accelerate your distribution or recover assets outside the official process. Any unsolicited contact offering to help with your Celsius claim in exchange for a fee should be treated as a potential scam and reported to Action Fraud (actionfraud.police.uk).

We will never ask for: your seed phrase, private key, exchange password, two-factor codes, remote access to your computer, or cryptocurrency payment to begin work. Any message claiming to be from CountWize and asking for these is a scam. Report it to us on the number above and to actionfraud.police.uk.
