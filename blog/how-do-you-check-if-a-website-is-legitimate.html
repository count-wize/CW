How do you check if a website is legitimate?

How can you check if a website is legit? There are simple and accessible ways to check the legitimacy of a website. There are mandatory actions when assessing the legality of any Internet resources. There are additional ways to check a website.

The Internet is a virtual space where people spend more time than in the real world. Every day, they visit hundreds of websites where they communicate, watch videos, read news, make purchases, exchange cryptocurrency and perform many other actions. At the same time, they rarely think about the risk of going to a page full of malicious links or an online resource where they can lose their money and personal data. To avoid such troubles, you must first check the website for legitimacy and only then click the mouse. You will learn how to conduct such a check from this article.

Reporting fraud: If you have been a victim of fraud, report it to Action Fraud (the UK's national reporting centre). Check if a firm is authorised at FCA ScamSmart.

How do you tell if a website is legitimate?

There are much fewer legitimate websites on the Internet than illegal ones. This unpleasant feature makes checking each visited online resource a mandatory task for the user. Various methods are used for these purposes, each of which makes it possible to detect fraud. However, the most effective is a comprehensive approach, in which the check is carried out according to several of the most important criteria. It can be done independently or with the help of a scam website checker. Both options make it possible to achieve the desired result, so choosing one of them always depends only on you.

Check URL

Illegal websites are often made to look the same as legal ones. Fraudsters copy everything, from the color scheme of the pages to the font used to write the text. By doing so, they mislead users and make them fall into a trap. At the same time, fraudsters cannot make the URL completely identical. If you study it carefully, you will find 1-2 differences from the original. The most common options are replacing letters, adding symbols, and using a different extension. Having discovered this, you can be sure that the website offered to you is illegal. In cases where you do not know the correct URL, you can use a scam URL checker, which will help identify an attempt at fraud.

SSL Certificates

An easy way to identify anillegal Internet resource is to conduct a website scammer check for the authenticity of SSL certificates. You can do this in any legal browser that you use. To check, you should find a lock icon on the line where the URL is written. By clicking on it, you will see information about the SSL certificate, indicating whether it is valid. Suppose instead of a lock, there is any other icon (for example, a triangle with an exclamation mark, a circle with the letter "i" inside, or a broken red lock). In that case, the SSL certificate is wholly or partially invalid. This means that the website being checked is illegal, and it is dangerous to go to it.

Scam website checker

The easiest and fastest way to check the legitimacy of any Internet resource is to use a scam website checker. This method allows you to identify deviations from standards and instantly expose the deception. Most often, a website legit checker is an online tool provided to users for free ora minimal fee. They only require entering the URL and starting the verification process. After completing these steps, you will have access to information about the SSL certificate, potential threats (e.g., malware, phishing, etc.),the security level of the website, and much more. In some cases, user reviews will also be provided.

Additional verification methods

Checking contacts

Supplementing the website check with a procedure for studying contact information is advisable. This information will be detailed, complete and open for legit Internet resources. This means the user can read the physical address, phone number, and e-mail linked to the domain at any time. All this data will be reliable (for example, the physical address can be found on a Google map, and you can make sure that the company whose website you are checking is registered at it), accurate and available for copying. The contact list should also contain links to profiles on several social networks.

Finding errors

All legit websites strive to provide users with reliable information. To do this, they repeatedly check the data used and update it. If the information is outdated or contains errors, such a website is most likely illegal. You should also know that on a legit Internet resource, the texts will have a minimum of grammatical, spelling and punctuation errors.

Amount of spam

Website spam score is a good way to check thelegitimacy of an Internet resource. It should show the absence of a large number of advertisements, pop-ups, and other aspects that interfere with the use of websites. If there is too much spam, someone wants to distract you and force you to perform various actions (for example, logging in to an account, entering personal data, etc.) without thinking about the possible consequences.

Studying privacy policy

Legit websites will always allow users to study privacy policies. It should be short and understandable to anyone who reads it. Also, this document should not contain complex phrases with the possibility of different interpretations and other suspicious aspects. If the privacy policy does not meet the listed criteria or does not exist at all, then such websites are most likely illegitimate.

Reading reviews

User reviews do not always reflect reality, but even by them, you can check the legit website. If you read them only on reliable platforms, you can get much useful information that helps to identify scammers or confirm the reliability of the Internet resource. At the same time, you should not forget the possibility of cheating reviews. Many positive comments about little-known websites, laconicism, and similarity in most reviews are clear signs of such a phenomenon.

Conclusion

Today, many illegal websites have been created on the Internet, where you can pick up a virus or lose money and personal data. Almost all of them are not very different from legitimate analogs, so it can be tough to identify fraud. However, using the verification methods described above and considering all the recommendations, you can protect yourself from dangerous websites and the actions of cybercriminals. For further guidance, see the National Cyber Security Centre's advice on shopping and browsing online securely.
