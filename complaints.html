Complaints Procedure

Last updated: 25 April 2026

CountWize Ltd is committed to providing a high standard of service. If you are dissatisfied with any aspect of our services, we want to hear from you so that we can put things right. This page explains how to raise a complaint, what we will do, and where you can escalate if you are not satisfied with our response.

CountWize Ltd is a company registered in England and Wales (company number 16198508), with its registered office at Unit 13 Freeland Park, Wareham Road, Lytchett Matravers, Poole BH16 6FA.

1

How to Raise a Complaint

You can raise a complaint with CountWize in two ways:

  • By email: complaints@countwize.co
  • By post: CountWize Ltd, Unit 13 Freeland Park, Wareham Road, Lytchett Matravers, Poole BH16 6FA

When raising a complaint, please include the following information so that we can investigate promptly:

  • Your full name and contact details
  • A clear description of your complaint
  • The date(s) of the issue and any relevant reference numbers
  • What outcome you are seeking
2

What We Will Do

CountWize takes all complaints seriously. When you raise a complaint, we commit to the following timescales:

  • Acknowledge your complaint within 5 business days of receipt.
  • Provide a full response within 15 business days of receipt.
  • Reach a final resolution or offer you an escalation route within 30 business days of receipt.

If we need more time to investigate your complaint, we will write to you to explain the reason for the delay and give you an updated timescale.

3

Escalation Routes

If you are not satisfied with our final response, or if we have not resolved your complaint within 30 business days, you may escalate your complaint to the following external bodies:

  • Trading Standards via the Citizens Advice consumer helpline: 0808 223 1133 (free from mobiles and landlines), or visit citizensadvice.org.uk.
  • Citizens Advice: citizensadvice.org.uk for general consumer guidance and support.

Important note regarding the Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS): CountWize Ltd is not authorised or regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA). Because we are not an FCA-authorised firm, complaints about our services cannot be escalated to the Financial Ombudsman Service. The appropriate escalation route for complaints about CountWize is Trading Standards via Citizens Advice, as described above.

4

FCA Non-Authorisation Statement

CountWize Ltd is not FCA-authorised. This website provides educational content and on-chain tracing services only. We do not provide financial advice, legal advice, or a guarantee of asset recovery.

Recovery outcomes depend on the specific circumstances of each case. If you have been a victim of fraud, report to actionfraud.police.uk. Check the regulatory status of any firm at fca.org.uk/scamsmart.

5

Governing Law

This complaints procedure is governed by the laws of England and Wales. Any disputes arising out of or in connection with this procedure shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.
