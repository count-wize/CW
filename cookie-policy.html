Cookie Policy

Last updated: 25 April 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how CountWize Ltd ("CountWize", "we", "us", or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on countwize.co ("Website"). It should be read alongside our Privacy Policy.

When you first visit the Website, a cookie banner will ask for your consent to non-essential cookies. You can change your preferences at any time by clearing your browser cookies and revisiting the site.

This Policy is issued in compliance with the Privacy and Electronic Communications (EC Directive) Regulations 2003 (PECR) and the UK General Data Protection Regulation (Retained Regulation (EU) 2016/679) ("UK GDPR"), as supplemented by the Data Protection Act 2018.

1

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, tablet, or smartphone) when you visit a website. They allow the site to recognise your device and remember certain information about your visit — such as your language preference or consent choices — to improve your experience on return visits.

Similar technologies such as pixel tags, web beacons, and local storage may be used for analogous purposes and are covered by this Policy.

2

How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Store your cookie consent preferences so we respect your choices on future visits.
  • Analyse website traffic and user behaviour to improve content and performance (Google Analytics 4).
  • Deliver and measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns (Google Ads and Meta Pixel).
  • Enable live chat support functionality (LiveChat).
  • Ensure the security and proper operation of the Website.

4

Your Cookie Choices

You have full control over non-essential cookies. You can manage your preferences in the following ways:

  • Cookie banner: when you first visit the Website, a banner lets you accept all, reject all, or customise which categories of cookies you allow.
  • Browser settings: most browsers allow you to view, block, or delete cookies. See the help section of your browser for instructions. Note that blocking all cookies may prevent some Website features from working.
  • Google opt-out: you can opt out of Google Analytics tracking via the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on, and manage Google Ads personalisation at adssettings.google.com.
  • Meta opt-out: you can manage Meta ad preferences at facebook.com/adpreferences.

To reset your cookie preferences on this Website, clear your browser cookies and revisit the site — the consent banner will reappear.

5

Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on this Website are placed by third-party services we use. These third parties set and access cookies in accordance with their own privacy policies:

  • Google LLC (United States) — Google Analytics 4 and Google Ads. Personal data transferred to the US under Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) with the UK International Data Transfer Addendum. See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Meta Platforms Ireland Ltd (Ireland) — Meta (Facebook) Pixel. The _fbp cookie is only set after you grant advertising consent via the cookie banner. See Meta's Privacy Policy.
  • LiveChat Software S.A. (Poland) — live chat support. Poland is an EU/EEA member state; data transfers are covered by the UK adequacy decision for the EEA. See LiveChat's Privacy Policy.

We do not control these third-party cookies and are not responsible for the content of their privacy policies. For details on international data transfers and safeguards, see our Privacy Policy.

Legal Basis

All other cookies — analytics, advertising, and functional — are set only after you give explicit consent via the cookie banner, in line with PECR and UK GDPR Article 6(1)(a).
